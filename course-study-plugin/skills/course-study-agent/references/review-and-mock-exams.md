# Review and Mock Exams

## Midterm or Final Review

1. Confirm the course and exam scope.
2. Prefer existing weekly/chapter notes and integrated notes.
3. Retrieve only relevant source sections; reread raw materials when notes are missing, unclear, or need verification.
4. Merge repeated concepts, connect topics, identify high-priority material, and create comparisons or formula/model summaries when useful.
5. Include uncertainty and a final review checklist.

## Past Papers and Mock Exams

1. Use past papers to infer question formats, mark allocation, topic distribution, wording, and approximate difficulty.
2. Generate new questions rather than copying a paper wholesale.
3. Adapt sections to the discipline: proofs, calculations, essays, cases, design tasks, code, data interpretation, or multiple choice as appropriate.
4. Separate questions from answers and explanations.
5. State that the mock exam reflects available evidence and does not predict the real exam.

Apply project-specific language, question count, format, and output-location rules when provided.
